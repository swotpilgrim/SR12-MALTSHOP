## Chapter 16: The Invitation

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Annie Palmer, first person  
**Setting:** Seabreeze Malt Shop  
**Time:** Late afternoon, three weeks into Chris's daily visits

**Purpose:** Chris asks Annie to Summer Social—midpoint turning point where private connection must become public, triggering Annie's crisis.

**Beats:**
1. Chris arrives seeming nervous, unusual for him. Orders cherry malt but doesn't drink it immediately. Makes small talk that feels like stalling. Annie notices his nervousness, asks what's wrong. Chris takes breath, then asks if she'd like to go to Seabreeze Summer Social with him.
2. Annie stunned into silence. Summer Social is town's biggest event—held at community center, attended by all prominent families, formal atmosphere, tradition. This isn't casual beach bonfire—this is public declaration. Annie's immediate response is "I can't," stumbling over explanations that don't quite make sense.
3. Chris disappointed, asks why not. Annie lists surface obstacles: doesn't have appropriate dress, would have to ask parents, not sure she'd fit in there, might have to work that evening. Chris addresses each objection patiently—he'll wait while she asks parents, dress can be found, Mr. Henderson would give her evening off, she'd fit in fine because she'd be with him.
4. But Annie's real fear unspoken—she's terrified of being exposed as not belonging in Chris's world. Boys like him take girls like Sandra Mitchell to events like Summer Social, not girls like Annie Palmer. Saying yes means stepping out of safety, risking public humiliation. Chris sees her genuine fear, doesn't push too hard but clearly hopes she'll say yes.
5. Chris tells Annie to think about it, leaves his number in case she decides yes. After he leaves, Betty and Ruth swarm Annie, excited and insistent she has to go. Annie overwhelmed—this is different from private malt shop conversations. This is official, public, a statement. Doesn't know if she's brave enough for that.

**Dialogue Topics:** Chris's nervous invitation, Annie's initial refusal and surface reasons, Chris addressing each practical obstacle, his invitation for her to think about it, Betty and Ruth's excited pressure.

**Emotional Arc:** Annie's terror outweighs her desire. Faced with making private connection public, all insecurities flood back. Wants to say yes but too afraid of exposure and humiliation.

**Key Details:** Summer Social as town's biggest event, formal atmosphere emphasized, Chris's phone number given, practical vs emotional obstacles, public declaration significance, sanctuary becoming insufficient.
